# Summary

The dot product measures how aligned two vectors are. Duality connects the dot product to linear transformations from space to the number line.

  

---

  
# 1. Dot Product

  

> When you multiply two vectors with the same dimension, where each row will be multiplied by the other one, then add the results together.

  

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = 1 \times 3 + 2 \times 4 = 11 \quad \text{(Projection of a vector)}$$

  

### Interpretation:

- If dot product is **positive** → vectors are in the **same direction**

- If dot product is **0** → vectors are **perpendicular**

- If dot product is **negative** → vectors are in **opposite directions**

  

$$\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

  

A **1×2 matrix** ↔ **2D vectors** → Matrix-vector product ⟺ Dot product

  

---

  

# 2. Duality

  

> Duality of a vector is the **linear transformation that it encodes** — a unique vector in which the linear transformation goes from space to the number line.
