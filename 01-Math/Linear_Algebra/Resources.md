# 📖 Resources — Linear Algebra

---

## 📚 Books

- [Mathematics for Machine Learning](https://mml-book.github.io/) — Ch. 2 & 3  
  A free book written specifically for ML practitioners. Chapters 2 and 3 cover vectors, matrices, and matrix decompositions in a clean, rigorous way. Great as a reference alongside a course.

---

## 🎓 Courses

- [Math for ML and Data Science Specialization](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/mathematics-for-machine-learning-and-data-science) — Course 1 · Coursera  
  Taught by DeepLearning.AI. The first course focuses entirely on linear algebra for ML — from vectors to eigenvalues — with a practical, hands-on approach using Python. The best structured starting point.

---

## 🌐 Free / Online

- [Essence of Linear Algebra](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab) — 3Blue1Brown  
  A visual, intuition-first YouTube series by Grant Sanderson. Doesn't teach computation — teaches you what linear algebra *means* geometrically. Watch this before or alongside anything else.

- [Linear Algebra](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/linear-algebra) — Khan Academy  
  Free, beginner-friendly, and well-paced. Good for building comfort with the mechanics — vectors, matrix operations, dot products — if you need to go slow and practice.

- [Linear Algebra](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06-linear-algebra-spring-2010/) — MIT OpenCourseWare  
  Gilbert Strang's legendary course. More rigorous and in-depth than what you need at the start, but invaluable once you want to go deeper. Lectures, problem sets, and exams all free.



## 🌐 Medium Articles

- [The Geometry of Uncertainty: Why Statistics is Actually Linear Algebra](https://medium.com/@tomkob99_89317/the-geometry-of-uncertainty-why-statistics-is-actually-linear-algebra-b525c008c31b) — Tomio Kobayashi 
  A short, eye-opening read that shows how core statistics concepts like mean, variance, and correlation are really just linear algebra in disguise — using geometric intuition (vectors, projections, decomposition) rather than formulas. Great for building the bridge between the two subjects.
